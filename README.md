# Biodiesel Production from Waste Cooking Oil and Neem Oil

## Overview
This project presents a minor-degree Chemical Engineering study on biodiesel production from waste cooking oil (WCO) and neem oil.

The work focused on producing biodiesel using the transesterification process and evaluating important fuel properties and emissions. The study explored waste cooking oil as a low-cost feedstock and also examined neem oil as a potential alternative source for biodiesel production.

## Objective
The main objective of the project was to produce biodiesel from waste cooking oil and neem oil and evaluate its fuel properties.

The work included:
- biodiesel production using transesterification
- viscosity testing
- flash and fire point testing
- calorific value measurement
- exhaust emission analysis
- comparison with diesel fuel

## Methodology
- Selected waste cooking oil and neem oil as biodiesel feedstocks
- Used methanol and KOH in the transesterification process
- Prepared biodiesel and separated glycerol from the final product
- Carried out fuel-property testing and performance comparison
- Evaluated the feasibility of biodiesel as an alternative fuel

## Tools / Equipment Used
- Magnetic stirrer
- Redwood viscometer
- Bomb calorimeter
- Flash and fire point apparatus
- Exhaust gas analyser
- Technical report writing and analysis

## Key Results
The study showed that biodiesel can be successfully produced from waste cooking oil and neem oil.

Reported results include:
- approximately **90% yield** of biodiesel from waste cooking oil
- calorific value of **37.18 MJ/kg** for WCO biodiesel
- calorific value of **28.23 MJ/kg** for neem oil biodiesel
- kinematic viscosity and flash/fire point values within a usable testing range
- lower carbon monoxide, particulate matter, and net carbon dioxide emissions compared with diesel

These results indicate that biodiesel from waste cooking oil has good potential as a lower-cost and more environmentally friendly alternative fuel.
